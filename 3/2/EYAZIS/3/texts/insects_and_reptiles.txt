Snakes are legless reptiles that slither across the ground. They swallow prey whole because they cannot chew food. Most snakes are not venomous but some can kill with a single bite. Snakes shed their skin several times each year as they grow.

Spiders have eight legs and produce silk from their bodies. They build webs to catch flying insects for food. A spider can make seven different types of silk. Most spiders are not dangerous to humans. They wait patiently for prey to become trapped in their webs.

Bees are important insects that pollinate flowers and make honey. A queen bee lays thousands of eggs each day. Worker bees collect nectar from flowers and bring it back to the hive. Bees communicate using a special dance to tell others where to find food.

Ants live in large colonies with millions of members. Each ant has a specific job to do for the group. They carry food many times their own weight. Ants leave chemical trails to help others find their way home. Some ants farm fungus for food inside their nests.

Butterflies start their lives as caterpillars crawling on plants. They transform into beautiful insects through metamorphosis. Butterflies taste with their feet to find the right plants for eggs. Their wings contain tiny scales that create colorful patterns.
