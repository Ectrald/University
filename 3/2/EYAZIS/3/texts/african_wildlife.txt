The African elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. These magnificent creatures live in herds led by the oldest female. The elephants use their trunks to drink water and grab food. Their large ears help them stay cool in hot climates. Baby elephants drink their mother's milk for two years.

Lions are known as the kings of the jungle. They hunt together in groups called prides. A lioness does most of the hunting while the male protects the territory. Lions can sleep for twenty hours each day. Their roar can be heard from eight kilometers away.

The blue whale is the biggest animal that has ever lived. These gentle giants eat tiny shrimp called krill. A baby blue whale drinks hundreds of liters of milk every day. Whales breathe air through a blowhole on their backs. They migrate thousands of kilometers each year.

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals. They communicate using clicks and whistles. Dolphins often swim alongside ships and play in the waves. They help each other when someone is injured. Baby dolphins stay with their mothers for several years.

The cheetah is the fastest land animal. It can run at speeds up to 113 kilometers per hour. These spotted cats hunt gazelles and other small animals. Cheetahs cannot roar but they purr like house cats. They live alone except during mating season.
