The domestic cat has lived with humans for thousands of years. Cats are independent animals that enjoy hunting mice and playing with toys. They purr when they are happy and meow to communicate with people. A cat spends many hours grooming its fur each day.

Dogs have been human companions for over fifteen thousand years. They are loyal animals that protect homes and help people in many ways. Some dogs guide blind people while others search for missing persons. Dogs wag their tails when they are happy to see you.

The horse is a strong and graceful animal used for transportation. Humans ride horses for sport and pleasure. Horses sleep standing up but they dream while lying down. A baby horse called a foal can run within hours of being born. Horses communicate using body language and sounds.

Rabbits are small mammals with long ears and fluffy tails. They eat carrots and lettuce and hop around gardens. Rabbits live in underground burrows called warrens. A mother rabbit feeds her babies once or twice each day. They can reproduce very quickly.

Turtles are reptiles that carry their homes on their backs. Some tortoises live for over one hundred years. They move slowly but never stop trying to reach their destination. Sea turtles travel thousands of kilometers to lay eggs on beaches.
