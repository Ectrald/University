The octopus has eight arms and three hearts. It lives in oceans around the world. An octopus can change color and texture to hide from predators. These intelligent creatures solve puzzles and open jars to get food. They squeeze through very small spaces because they have no bones.

Sharks have been swimming in the oceans for millions of years. They have multiple rows of sharp teeth that constantly fall out and grow back. Most sharks do not attack humans but prefer to eat fish and seals. A shark never stops moving because it must breathe to survive.

Jellyfish drift through the water without a brain or heart. They sting their prey using tentacles covered with special cells. Some jellyfish glow in the dark at night. A group of jellyfish is called a smack. They have been on Earth for over five hundred million years.

The seahorse is a tiny fish with a horse-shaped head. It swims upright and uses its tail to grab seaweed. Male seahorses carry babies in a special pouch on their bodies. They hunt tiny shrimp and plankton every day. Seahorses are monogamous and mate for life.

Crabs walk sideways using their ten legs. They have strong claws for fighting and cracking shells. Hermit crabs live inside empty shells to protect their soft bodies. Crabs breathe through gills and must stay wet to survive. Some crabs can regenerate lost limbs.
