Birds are warm-blooded animals with feathers. They lay eggs and most of them can fly. The eagle has excellent eyesight and dives at great speed to catch fish. Owls hunt at night using their sharp claws and silent wings. Penguins cannot fly but they swim beautifully underwater.

Parrots are colorful birds that can mimic human speech. They live in tropical forests and eat fruits and seeds. Some parrots live for over eighty years. They use their feet to hold food while eating. Parrots are very social and intelligent creatures.

Sparrows are small birds that live near humans. They eat seeds and insects and build nests in small spaces. These birds sing beautiful songs at dawn. Sparrows travel in flocks searching for food together. They adapt easily to different environments.

The hummingbird is the smallest bird in the world. It can hover in mid-air and fly backwards. Hummingbirds drink nectar from flowers using their long beaks. Their wings beat over fifty times per second. These tiny birds visit hundreds of flowers every day.

Owls have special feathers that make no sound when they fly. They turn their heads almost all the way around. Owls swallow their prey whole and later spit out pellets of bones. Many cultures consider owls to be symbols of wisdom.
